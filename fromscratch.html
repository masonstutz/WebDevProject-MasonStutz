My Favorite Films

Five movies that stuck with me — and why they matter

Why Movies?

I love movies. Good storytelling is something I thoroughly enjoy. For me, the thing that makes a movie great is transformative acting, clever writing, good directing, and a memorable score. The films below are some of my favorites; they all have something that stuck with me long after the credits rolled.

The Films

  1. The Shining (1980) — Stanley Kubrick
    • Genre: Psychological Horror
    • Stars Jack Nicholson in one of cinema's most unhinged performances
    • The Overlook Hotel is practically its own character
    • Kubrick's use of the Steadicam changed filmmaking forever
  2. Good Will Hunting (1997) — Gus Van Sant
    • Genre: Drama
    • Written by Matt Damon and Ben Affleck — who also star in it
    • Robin Williams' therapy scenes are some of the best in any film
    • "It's not your fault" hits differently every time
  3. Marty Supreme (2025) — Jonah Hill
    • Genre: Sports Drama
    • Timothée Chalamet plays ping-pong prodigy Marty Reisman
    • Shot on 16mm film for a raw, gritty aesthetic
    • A love letter to obsession and competitive drive
  4. Interstellar (2014) — Christopher Nolan
    • Genre: Sci-Fi / Drama
    • Hans Zimmer's organ-driven score is one of the best ever made
    • The docking scene is pure tension without a single word of dialogue
    • Made me cry in an IMAX theater in front of strangers
  5. La La Land (2016) — Damien Chazelle
    • Genre: Musical Romance
    • Emma Stone and Ryan Gosling have effortless chemistry
    • The opening freeway musical number was filmed in two days
    • The ending is an emotional gut punch disguised as a love story

What Makes a Great Film?

In my opinion, the best movies share a few traits:

  • A story that excites emotions
  • At least one scene you can't stop thinking about days later
  • A score or soundtrack that does half the storytelling
  • Characters who feel real, even if the world around them isn't
